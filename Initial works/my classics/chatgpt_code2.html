"Gravity Draws Me Down"

Gravity draws me down, ooh ooh, oohooh ooh,
Feet on the pavement, dreams overdue.
Skyscrapers weep in electric rain,
Everyone running from something they can't name.

That girl on the corner hums to survive,
Sings to the night just to feel alive.
I pass her by, she fades from view,
But in her song, I hear me too.

A man in a suit with bloodshot eyes,
Holds a bouquet like a thin disguise.
Is it for love, or is it goodbye?
I’ll never know, just walk on by.

Sonder hits like a midnight train,
Every face holds joy and pain.
I’m just a line in a bigger script,
A background blur in someone’s trip.

A child laughs on a broken swing,
While a mother hides her wedding ring.
Stories crash like waves in the street,
Each heartbeat drumming its own beat.

Gravity draws me down — not just to ground,
But into the lives that spin around.
A million souls with burning skies,
Carrying truths behind their eyes.

And though I’m small, I start to see,
They don’t know me — and that sets me free.
In their chaos, I find my place,
A shadow dancing in shared space.

So sing it soft, or shout it loud,
We're all just faces in the crowd.
But every one a world profound —
And that’s what draws me down.
